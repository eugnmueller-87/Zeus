# Macro Playbooks — How to Trade Each Market Regime

## Bull Market (SP500 1m return > +2%, VIX < 20)
Characteristics: trending higher, dip-buying works, risk appetite high, bad news ignored.

Playbook:
- Favour LONG trades strongly. Short-selling in a bull market requires exceptional conviction.
- Positive signals (partnerships, earnings beats, product launches) have high hit rates.
- Supply chain disruptions are buyable dips if the disruption is temporary and company is fundamentally strong.
- Position sizing: use upper range (3-5% per trade). Market is forgiving.
- Stop-loss: can use wider stops (4%) since volatility is lower and trending.
- Sectors to favour: tech, consumer discretionary, industrials.

Signal multiplier: confidence × 1.2 (bull market bonus).

## Bear Market (SP500 1m return < -3% OR VIX > 35)
Characteristics: downtrend, rallies sold, risk-off, bad news amplified, good news ignored.

Playbook:
- Favour SHORT trades or cash. Long trades require extreme conviction and must be in defensive sectors.
- Supply chain disruption signals have very high hit rates for shorts.
- Positive news signals are traps — the market will fade them. SUPPRESS positive signals in bear + high VIX.
- Regulatory action signals: amplified downside, strong short candidates.
- Position sizing: use lower range (1-2% per trade). Capital preservation is priority.
- Stop-loss: tighter (2-2.5%) because moves are fast and brutal.
- Sectors to favour: energy, utilities, healthcare, consumer staples. Avoid speculative tech.

Signal multiplier: confidence × 0.8 (bear market penalty on longs, × 1.3 on shorts).

## Sideways/Range Market (VIX 15-25, SP500 flat)
Characteristics: oscillating between support and resistance, mean reversion works, trend-following fails.

Playbook:
- Trade only high-conviction signals (severity CRITICAL or HIGH with strong Hades compliance).
- Both longs and shorts viable but smaller size.
- Positive signals near range highs → skip. Disruption signals near range lows → skip.
- Position sizing: default 2%.
- Favour earnings surprises and regulatory actions over trend-following signals.

## High Volatility (VIX 25-35)
Characteristics: elevated fear, whipsaw moves, both directions see big swings.

Playbook:
- Reduce all position sizes by 50%.
- Require CRITICAL severity minimum to trade.
- Widen stops slightly (3.5-4%) to survive volatility spikes.
- Best opportunities: supply chain disruptions (people panic-sell, then stabilise).
- Avoid earnings plays — moves are unpredictable even if direction is right.

## Extreme Volatility (VIX > 35) — ALL SIGNALS SUPPRESSED
VIX above 35 indicates systemic fear (financial crisis, geopolitical shock).
No signal has reliable predictive value in this environment.
ZEUS suppresses all signals until VIX drops below 30 on a 3-day average.
Cash is a position. Preservation of capital is the only objective.

## Sector Momentum Context
Always check sector ETF momentum before trading a signal:
- Tech signal in a -5% tech sector month → headwind, reduce size
- Energy disruption signal in a +8% energy sector month → tailwind, increase size
- If sector momentum contradicts the signal direction, require confidence > 0.70 to proceed
